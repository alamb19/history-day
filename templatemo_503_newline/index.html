    The AIDS Epidemic

    What is The Aids Epidemic

    In the early 1980s a case of AIDS broke out and began to take the lives of many, a large portion of the victims were gay men, causing people to fear gay men on account of many of them carrying the terminal disease. Victims like Ryan White forced the government to finally come forward and confront the issue, leading to more awareness and soon a higher probability of survival.

  • Timeline

    The AIDS Epidemic

    Timeline

    1980-1981- 5 young actively homosexual men, all previously healthy, were treated for Pneumocystis carinii pneumonia, and were tested having cytomegalovirus (CMV) infection and candidal mucosal infection.

    1981- the CDC receives numerous cases of similar reports and infections among gay men, including Kaposi’s Sarcoma, an aggressive cancer.

    1982- CDC uses the term “AIDS” (Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome)

    1982- CDC reports a case of AIDS in an infant who received blood transfusions.

    1983- The world’s first dedicated outpatient AIDS clinic opens in San Francisco General Hospital.

    1983- the CDC reported cases of women with AIDS from male partners.

    1985- Ryan White is refused entry to his school for contracting aids in a blood transfusion. His family takes legal action to protect Ryan’s rights to attend school.

    1987- AIDS activist Cleve Jones creates the first panel of the memorial quilt.

    1987- AIDS quilt is officially created and formally organized the NAMES project foundation.

    1988- Ryan White testifies before the president on his AIDS commission.

    1990- Ryan White passes away due to AIDS related illness.

    1991- Magic Johnson announces that he has aids.

    1992- AIDS becomes the #1 cause of death in males aged 25-44.

    1993- the first major Hollywood film, Philadelphia, involving AIDS in the main character is released.

    1994- AIDS becomes the #1 cause of death in all Americans aged 25-44.

    1996- The number of new AIDS cases in the US declines for the first time since the beginning of the epidemic.

    Compromise

    In the year 1996 the number of new AIDS cases in the US declines for the first time since the beginning of the epidemic. Since then the medical knowledge of AIDS has become greater and the people fatally suffering has become lesser. In 1997 the rate of death went down by 47% compared to the previous year.
